# Core Loop
When you enter a round, you get a selection of starter *turrets* (weapons). When introduction is over, the player starts the first wave of enemies of the 2 in the *battlefield* (stage). In-between the waves the player is given *scrap*, which can be used to "craft" (buy with scrap) other turrets and *augmentations* (abilities). After the second wave ends, the player starts a *juggernaut* (boss) fight, which, upon being killed triggers another option to craft, and after some time switches stages. The process is repeated several more times *(probably ~4-5 more)* until the fight with a *dreadnought* (final boss).

# Themes
The central themes of **A:RC** are *conquering* and *destruction*. The player is one of the many *machines* (battle cars) airdropped onto a planet with the intent of capturing it from another megacorp (more on that later) for its later extortion for resources, in the process destroying any military bases, drills, frackers, etc. set up by the enemy megacorp.

# Primary Mechanics
The primary mechanics of the game are, of course, driving and shooting:
- Driving is unique in this game, in the sense that the machine *can't drive straight*. The player can only tell the machine to start steering left or right, but there's no such thing as "forwards" or "backwards". The player can also *drift*, increasing the machine's angular momentum, but decreasing its linear velocity, as well as slightly inclining the player's orientation towards the center of the circle formed by the car's angular movement to encourage the player to drift while shooting.
- Holding the shoot key will make you shoot the turret *(who could've possibly known?)*. The game doesn't have any "ammo", meaning the player can shoot as many bullets as the player likes.

The player and enemy machines only have *1 HP*. Juggernauts and dreadnoughts have multiple HP (depends on playtest results).
The player technically, just like the enemies, only has 1 HP, but, unlike the enemies, the player machine also has a *reflector* (shield), which also breaks from one hit, but unlike HP, the reflector regenerates after a specific amount of time had passed (yet unsure how long, will depend on the results of playtests and difficulty settings).
Augmentations, like stated earlier, are secondary abilities distinct from turrets. Augmentations have many different variations, for example a tether augmentation that allows the player to reel in an enemy machine, or hit another machine with the hooked machine, causing both to be destroyed. That's just one example of many to be implemented.
